2016

Continued work at Doctor Evidence as the UI Lead. Converted all front-end components to new functional-reactive framework. Routinely audited new features and implementations for performance. Created mockups for all new features and pages. Performed user interviews and research to scope out new features. Hiring, onboarding, and assessment of new front-end developers.

Created a development blog and wrote articles describing my visualization and UX work and on quality communication in the remote environment.

2015

Hired full-time at Doctor Evidence as a senior front-end developer. Built six multi-step wizards for filtering and selecting data then reporting results in a highly configurable format. Converted entire codebase from CoffeeScript to ES6. Researched and implemented new tooling for project management and release/deployment tracking. Architechted new front-end data model for vastly improved performance.

2014

Started as a fully remote independent contractor for Doctor Evidence, building out the front-end for a brand new single-page application targeted towards medical researchers, pharmacologists, and biostatisticians. Established the platform-wide style and aesthetic guide, wrote all CSS, created all reusable front-components, and constructed several interactive visualizations in d3.

Began exploring creative coding projects leveraging canvas, SVG, and Processing.

2013

Hired as a developer-designer for tech consulting firm in Kansas City, MO. Created a custom eCommerce site for Medicare supplies. Designed and implemented the site structure, shopping cart, and purchasing interface. Wrote all copy, marketing/promotional materials, and operated AdWords campaigns.

Started creating digital artwork with a focus on abstract, geometric, and generative styles.

2012

Hired as a data assistant for the publishing arm of the Ecological Society of America in Ithaca, NY. Taught myself PHP and SQL in order to automate most of the operations in my work, and redesigned their website and tutorials in response to frequent problems encountered during communication with authors.

2003

Started a personal blog, beginning the journey of learning HTML and CSS. Created a variety of hobby websites while developing skills in all aspects of technology - building and repairing computers, business IT administration and server maintenance, and technical support for all types of consumer electronics.

I'm Tim, a web developer, technologist, and all-around internet enthusiast based in Brooklyn, NY. I've been building websites of all shapes and sizes for fifteen years, with a focus on rendering complex information and designing intuitive and sophisticated interfaces.

Over my career, I have witnessed the fatal disconnect between the design of our technology and the needs of our users. I craft tools that bridge this divide: intuitive software that enhances our creative and intellectual abilities. My dream is to close the gap between thought and creation.

As a developer and designer. I've worked directly with users, clients, and stakeholders to draft specifications and mockups, implemented these requests and iterated based on feedback, tracked usage and behavior to identify failures and successes, and reported these results back to stakeholders for review. I believe in a holistic approach to development - I will take on any task necessary that will achieve the best result for my users.

If you share my enthusiasm for building powerful, intuitive software, I'd love to talk to you.
