2017

Promoted to UI Lead at Doctor Evidence, responsible for the design and implementation of all front-end components and features across multiple independent applications, also contributing to project management and hiring/onboarding of new front-end developers.

Created a development blog and wrote articles describing my visualization and UX work and on quality communication in the remote environment.

2015

Hired full-time at Doctor Evidence as a senior front-end developer, where I architected and implemented a new front-end data model for clinical research trials. I continued my work on visualizations, refactored the entire codebase from CoffeeScript to ES6, and built dozens of new tools and features for generating reports, sorting/filtering/searching large quantities of data, and assisted in project management.

Rebuilt my blog with a completely new style. More responsive design for better mobile display, better contrast for legibility.

2014

Hired as a fully remote independent contractor for Doctor Evidence, building out the front-end for a brand new single-page application targeted towards medical researchers, pharmacologists, and biostatisticians. Established the platform-wide style and aesthetic guide, wrote all CSS, created all reusable front-components, and constructed several interactive visualizations in d3.

Began exploring creative coding projects leveraging canvas, SVG, and Processing.

2013

Hired as a developer-designer for tech consulting firm in Kansas City, MO. Created a custom eCommerce site for Medicare supplies, where I designed and implemented the site structure, shopping cart, and purchasing interface. I also wrote all copy, marketing/promotional materials, and operated AdWords campaigns.

Started creating digital artwork with a focus on abstract, geometric, and generative styles. Made my first creative/artistic portfolio site.

Created my first development portfolio site.

2012

Hired as a data assistant for the publishing arm of the Ecological Society of America in Ithaca, NY. I taught myself PHP and SQL to automate most of the operations in my work, and redesigned the ESA website and tutorials in response to frequent problems encountered during communication with authors.

Created a variety of hobby websites, including a small informational site for a friend's sewing business.

Started a personal blog, beginning the journey of learning HTML and CSS.

2003

I'm Tim, a web developer, technologist, and all-around internet enthusiast based in Brooklyn, NY. I've been building websites of all shapes and sizes for fifteen years, with a focus on rendering complex information and designing intuitive and sophisticated interfaces.

Over my career, I have witnessed the fatal disconnect between the design of our technology and the needs of our users. I craft tools that bridge this divide: intuitive software that enhances our creative and intellectual abilities. My dream is to close the gap between thought and creation.

As a developer and designer. I've worked directly with users, clients, and stakeholders to draft specifications and mockups, implemented these requests and iterated based on feedback, tracked usage and behavior to identify failures and successes, and reported these results back to stakeholders for review. I believe in a holistic approach to development - I will take on any task necessary that will achieve the best result for my users.

If you share my enthusiasm for building powerful, intuitive software, I'd love to talk to you.
